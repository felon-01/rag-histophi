The Cold War (1947â€“1991) was a geopolitical rivalry between the United States and the Soviet Union. It involved ideological conflict between capitalism and communism but avoided direct large-scale warfare.

Key events included the Berlin Blockade, Korean War, Cuban Missile Crisis, Vietnam War, and the arms race. Nuclear deterrence, espionage, and proxy conflicts defined the era.
